On an American seafaring vessel, seawater may encompass the ship, but fresh water is a scarce commodity. To protect the health of crewmembers onboard the ship, water conservation is put into place. The individual's shower time must be limited. Therefore, the Navy Shower was developed to conserve water and benefit each crewmember. Through reduction of waste, the Navy Shower allows the entire community to benefit from each sailor's obligation.

1

Prepare shower with soap, shampoo, and a towel.

2

After preparing supplies, remove clothing.

3

Open curtain and step into shower.

4

Turn on shower head. The shock of cold water is very short-lived.

5

For 15 seconds, coat body with water. Use hands or a washcloth to fully douse body.

6

Turn off water and continue to lather skin

7

Rinse off the soap and shampoo with water for 20 more seconds.

8

Turn off water and remove excess soap. Step out of shower.

9

Dry off skin and remnants of wet hair. Water should be in the shower, not on the rest of the floor.

10

Once finished, put on clothing, gather belongings, and leave shower.
