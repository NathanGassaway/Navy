• On an American seafaring vessel, seawater may encompass the ship, but fresh water is a scare commodity. To protect the health of crewmembers onboard the ship, water conservation is put into place. The individual's shower time must be limited. Therefore, the Navy Shower was developed to conserve water and benefit each crewmember. Through reduction of waste, the Navy Shower allows the entire community to benefit from each sailor's obligation.

  • 1

    Prepare shower with soap, shampoo, and a towel.

  • 2

    After preparing supplies, remove clothing.

  • 3

    Open and step into shower.

  • 4

    Turn on shower head. The shock of cold water is very short-lived.

  • 5

    For 15 seconds, coat body with water. Use hands or a washcloth to fully douse body.

  • 6

    Turn off water and continue to lather skin for 30 seconds.

  • 7

    Rinse off the soap and shampoo with water for 20 more seconds.

  • 8

    Turn off water and remove ids soap. Step class="description"out of shower.
